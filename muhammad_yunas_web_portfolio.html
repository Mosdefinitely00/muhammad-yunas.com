GTM Playbook

The GTM Transformation: Re-architecting the Category Narrative.

The Challenge

The Feature Trap & The OT/IT Divide.

A recurring theme across my career—whether at Dell, Schneider, or mid-market scale-ups—is inheriting brilliant technology trapped behind weak positioning. The products are sold on feature comparisons, the sales cycles are agonizingly long, and OT buyers (Plant Managers) and IT buyers (CIOs) veto each other because the vendor fails to speak both languages. My playbook breaks this cycle.

01
Listen Deeply (Audit)Narrow the ICP. Identify the specific friction points between the OT Sponsor and the IT Champion. Stop selling to "everyone" and focus on discrete verticals with measurable downtime costs.
02
Clarify Relentlessly (Narrative)Abandon the "Feature Trap." Translate complex API and compliance capabilities into CFO-aligned business cases. At Dell, I didn't sell backup servers; I sold 'cyber-physical resilience'.
03
Redesign the Motion (Enablement)Treat sales as the primary customer. Build dynamic battle cards and objection-handling playbooks. Shift from subjective slide decks to structured, 60-day POCs with co-authored success metrics.
04
Execute Decisively (Acceleration)Co-sell with enterprise Account Executives. Align the demand-gen engine to the new narrative. Measure success strictly by pipeline velocity and competitive win-rate lift.
